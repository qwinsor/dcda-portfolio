AI Tool Evaluation

Introduction

For lab 2 of this semesters work I have decided to evaluate the two AI engines Notebook Lm and Leonardo AI. I thought this would be an interesting comparison because they serve completely different purposes. One being an image generator and another aimed toward helping individuals learn.

Tool 1: Leonardo Ai

Capabilities

As the name suggests this tool is very good at creating images. There are many styles, aspect ratios and forms of media that this tool can generate so it leaves you with a lot of options to explore. It seems to also have different models you can choose from to achieve different styles of art including some decent videos. I found that most of the images generated served their purpose well, and as long as I gave a prompt that the AI could work with, the image was fine. However, there happened to be a LOT of things that this tool could not achieve. For starters, this tool can't do very much research. For example, when I asked it to make a picture of Anakin Skywalker using the force, it had no idea what that was or how to show it. It couldn't make an image with Batman using his gadgets, and it couldnt make an image teaching me anything about drilling oil, other than what a pump and oil crew looked like. It also wasnt very robust, meaning if I gave it the same prompt twice, they would be completely different images in about half of the cases. When typed in "write an essay about drilling oil" it spit out two different images of the same oil rig and crew, however one was cartoon style and the other was realistic. When I did the same with "TCU hockey game, make no players wear number 7" it first showed me a 300's distance of an unrecogniable organization (with multiple players wearing number 7) then it showed a weird picture depicting a TCU hockey player with a Giant X over it. This ties into its next limitation which is that it cannot handle following contrains very well. If I were to give it a specific thing that it should not do, it would almost always do exactly that. I would have loved to have had more time to see just how far I could push Leonardo AI, however it limited me to 20 prompts. Unfortunate.

Appropriate Use

Some of the cases in which I would definately use this product are if I need to make a quick peice of art that generally gets the idea across. Things like scenes in nature, charecters of fiction or history, or objects are great. I would imagine if I were to throw a themed party This tool would be great for some posters. I would not use it for anything informative. It couldn't do any real research, and its facts are often wrong unless they are surface level things like batmans appearance. /p>

Ethical Considerations

Unfortunatly tools like this can be used for all kinds of horrible things. Scams, deepfakes, propaganda, you name it. This issue started arrising many years ago when this technology first came out, and now its been refined to the point that some of these images and videos look more real than videos from the early 2000's. I did not test any of these unethical capabilities because I obviously don't agree with that, but I have seen plenty of pretty awful things on social media. That being said it comes down the the users of these tools, and hope that everyone will choose to do the right thing.

Tool 2: Notebook LM

Capabilities

Notebook LM is an AI tool that I have used a few times in the past. It is targeted at making learning any topic much easier. It does this by asking you for an imput, meaning type what you want to learn about, then it lists off a number of sources for you to sign off on, then it compiles all that information to create one of many modes of learning. The surface level options on the imput side are "deep research mode" which makes it think for a lot longer, you can choose sources from a google drive, and also add sources yourself. On the output side the options are: Audio overview, video overview, mind map, Reports, flashcards, Quiz, infographic, slide deck, and data table. This gives you a somewhat wide range of options, however there isn't much room for non-learning based actions. One thing I noticed right away was that it takes a lot of time to make any of these outputs so I found myself just waiting around a lot which is not a good thing when I am trying to study. That being said you can generate multiple modes of study at once so I can study a mind map while I am waiting for the video overview. The video overviews and other content works unbelievably well when the prompt was something straight forward. For example when I typed in "Oil Drilling" the mind map, and video that I asked for were almost perfect. The video was filled with infographics and a voice to walk you through the lesson. The fun started to happen when I started asking it to generate images for me. For example, "make a picture of Batman fighting Anakin Skywalker". It was no longer able to create things like infographics or video essays, but it could produce quizes and flashcards on what the people of the internet have previously stated. Next I wanted to see how accurate the webite is, so I asked it to tech me something that I already knew about. For this I chose "Recent History of the NHL". I generated an infographic, a mind map, a quiz, and a data table (just to see what would happen). To my surprise, Notebook LM was able to make a data table and a mind map of the recent history of the nhl, but the infographic and the quiz had absolutely nothing to do with the subject at all. After doing all of this i determined that this AI is good at its job, but the user has to do a lot of work with filtering in or out sources, and even still you might not get what you are looking for.

Appropriate Use

Notebook LM does accomplish the goal that it sets out to do, which is teach the user about a subject of their choosing. I would say this is one of the best tutoring tools I have ever seen. That being said it is still a little faulty, and you can't do anything else with it. Making images, messing around for fun, even making a calander for one's homework is impossible.

Ethical Considerations

I think this is the most ethical use of AI to date. It is what AI is supposed to be used for. It doesnt replace any necessary cognitive thinking except for the tedious creation of these study tools, and it encourages human learning. The only problem I can see is that if this technology continues to grow, the need for tutors and some levels of teaching may be abolished, thereby putting some people out of work.

Broader Reflections

Both of these tools can be incredibly useful to people for different reasons. Either learning or generating images. I will certainly be using them both for those reasons. I think that the Leonardo AI will be mostly for fun because anything it generates will be unusable for academic purposes.
